**Best Accounting Software for Sole Traders UK (2026 Guide)**

**Introduction**

If you're a sole trader in the UK right now, picking accounting software isn't just a convenience decision — it's increasingly a compliance one. From April 2026, Making Tax Digital for Income Tax (MTD ITSA) requires sole traders earning over £50,000 to keep digital records and submit quarterly updates to HMRC through approved software. The £30,000 threshold follows in April 2027, and £20,000 in 2028. That means the spreadsheet-and-annual-return approach many sole traders still rely on has a firm expiry date.

The good news: the software market has caught up, and there are genuinely strong options at every price point — including free. The less good news: the differences between tools matter more than marketing materials suggest, and choosing one that doesn't fit your workflow will quietly cost you time every single week.

This guide covers five tools that are worth your attention as a UK sole trader in 2026 — what they're actually good at, where they fall short, and who should choose each one. All pricing has been verified against live provider websites.

  

**⚡** **Quick Verdict**

**Best for most sole traders:** **FreeAgent** — particularly if you bank with NatWest, RBS, or Ulster Bank, where it's free. It was built for people in exactly your situation, handles Self Assessment and MTD natively, and is straightforward enough that you won't need an accounting degree to use it. For everyone else, the right choice depends on whether you're VAT-registered, whether you work with an accountant, and how complex your finances are.

The Simple plan is a relatively new addition specifically aimed at non-VAT-registered sole traders and landlords, with MTD for Income Tax baked in from the start. At the Ignite level and above, Xero is among the most complete platforms available. **Who should choose Xero:** Sole traders who already work with an accountant (who likely uses Xero), those expecting to grow — hire staff, incorporate, or take on international clients — and anyone who needs a wide range of app integrations. If you're a graphic designer invoicing three clients a month, Xero is probably more than you need. If you're a consultant with international clients and a growth trajectory, it's the right foundation.

**Who should choose FreshBooks:** Service-based sole traders who invoice regularly and bill by the hour — creatives, consultants, coaches, copywriters. Anyone who finds other tools too accounting-heavy and wants software that prioritises the client-facing side of the business. Not ideal for managing your own Self Assessment, or for sole traders who need strong UK tax reporting natively.

**FAQ**

**1. What is MTD ITSA and do I need to worry about it as a sole trader?** MTD for Income Tax Self Assessment (MTD ITSA) is HMRC's requirement for sole traders to keep digital financial records and submit quarterly updates rather than a single annual return. From April 2026, it applies to sole traders with income over £50,000; from April 2027, over £30,000; and from April 2028, over £20,000. All tools on this list are on HMRC's approved software list for MTD ITSA, but features vary — check whether quarterly submission is included on your specific plan tier before signing up.

**2. Can I use free software and still be MTD-compliant?** Yes. FreeAgent is free for NatWest/RBS/Ulster Bank customers and fully MTD ITSA-compliant. Sage Sole Trader is free for non-VAT-registered sole traders and also MTD ITSA-ready. Both are on HMRC's approved list.

**3. Do I need an accountant if I use accounting software?** Not necessarily, but it depends on how confident you are with your finances. Software handles the admin and compliance mechanics; an accountant provides tax planning, checks your figures, and can save you more than they cost in avoided errors. If you're newly self-employed with straightforward finances, you can manage solo — especially with FreeAgent or FreshBooks. As income grows or your situation becomes more complex, an accountant is worth the investment.

**4. What happens to my data if I cancel my subscription?** This varies significantly by provider. Xero, QuickBooks, and Sage allow data exports. FreeAgent data remains accessible after cancellation in read-only form. FreshBooks is the most restrictive — cancelling your subscription means losing access to your records, so export everything before you leave.

**5. Which tool is best if I have international clients or invoice in foreign currencies?** Xero is the strongest option for multi-currency work, available on the Comprehensive plan (£50/month). QuickBooks Essentials (£28/month) also supports multiple currencies. FreeAgent and FreshBooks handle international invoicing, but multi-currency accounting is limited. If a meaningful part of your income comes from overseas clients, factor this into your plan choice early — it's harder to switch later once you have transaction history.
