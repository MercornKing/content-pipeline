# Monday.com Review for Small Business

## Introduction

If you've been trying to wrangle a growing business with a combination of shared spreadsheets, sticky notes, WhatsApp threads, and the occasional frantic email chain, someone has probably mentioned Monday.com to you. It's one of the most heavily advertised project management tools around — you can't watch five minutes of YouTube without seeing the ad — and it promises to be the one place where your work, your team, and your projects all live together.

For a small business owner or freelancer in the UK, that pitch is genuinely appealing. Keeping track of client work, internal tasks, deadlines, and team capacity across multiple tools is exhausting. The question isn't whether you need something better — you probably do. The question is whether Monday.com specifically is the right answer, or whether the marketing spend has built a reputation the product can't quite justify for smaller operations.

This review covers what Monday.com actually does well, where it falls short for small businesses, what it costs in real terms, and who it's genuinely suited to. No fluff, no affiliate-driven cheerleading — just an honest assessment.

---

## Quick Verdict

**Good tool, but not always the right fit for very small teams.**

Monday.com is a genuinely capable platform with excellent visual project tracking, strong automations, and impressive flexibility. But its pricing model — which requires a minimum of three seats — and the sheer breadth of features can make it feel like overkill (and overpriced) for solo operators or very small teams. If you have three or more people and want a polished, scalable system, it's worth serious consideration.

## What Is Monday.com?

Monday.com is a cloud-based work operating system — its own term — that lets you build boards to track projects, tasks, clients, campaigns, pipelines, or just about anything else you can organise in rows and columns. It launched in 2012, went public on Nasdaq in 2021, and has grown into one of the dominant players in the project management space alongside tools like Asana, ClickUp, and Notion.

The core concept is simple: you create a board, add items (essentially rows), and assign columns to track whatever information matters — status, owner, deadline, priority, budget, files. You then view that board as a table, a kanban board, a Gantt chart, a calendar, or a timeline, depending on what you need to see. Different team members can work from the same data presented in the view that suits them best.

On top of that foundation, Monday.com layers in automations, integrations, dashboards, workdocs (a built-in document editor), and a CRM module. It's a platform as much as a product, which is both its appeal and, at times, its problem.

## Key Features

**Boards and Views**

The board system is where Monday.com earns its reputation. Building a project board is fast and intuitive — drag columns, rename status labels, colour-code priorities — and the range of views means you're not locked into one way of looking at your work. The Gantt view is particularly useful for client project timelines, and the calendar view helps with deadline management without needing a separate tool.

Column types are extensive: text, numbers, dates, dropdowns, people, files, links, ratings, time tracking, and more. This flexibility means the same board structure can work for a marketing campaign, a client onboarding process, or a product launch without needing to compromise.

**Automations**

Monday.com's automation builder is one of its strongest features and accessible enough that you don't need a technical background to use it. You can set up rules along the lines of "when status changes to Done, notify the project owner" or "when a deadline passes, send a reminder to the assigned person" using a simple if/then interface.

There are also pre-built automation recipes for common workflows, which speed things up considerably. More complex automations — branching logic, multi-step sequences — are available on higher plans. For most small business use cases, the basics available on mid-tier plans are sufficient.

**Integrations**

Monday.com connects with a wide range of tools: Slack, Google Workspace, Microsoft Teams, Zoom, Outlook, Salesforce, HubSpot, Jira, GitHub, Zapier, and many others. For UK businesses using Google Workspace or Microsoft 365, the integrations are functional and reasonably straightforward to set up.

It's worth noting that the depth of integrations varies. Some are genuinely two-way syncs; others are essentially one-directional triggers. Check the specifics of any integration you're counting on before committing.

**Dashboards**

Dashboards pull data from multiple boards into a single overview — useful for a business owner who wants to see project status, team workload, and upcoming deadlines across everything at once. You can build widgets for task counts, progress tracking, budget summaries, and more.

On lower-tier plans, dashboards can only pull from a limited number of boards. On higher plans, there's no cap. If cross-board visibility is important to you, factor this into which plan you need.

**monday CRM**

Monday.com has expanded into CRM territory with a dedicated CRM product that sits within the same platform. For a small business that wants to manage leads, deals, and client communication without a separate CRM subscription, it's a credible option. It's not as feature-rich as dedicated CRM tools like HubSpot or Pipedrive, but for basic pipeline management it works well and the integration with your existing Monday.com boards is seamless.

**Workdocs**

A relatively recent addition, Workdocs is Monday.com's answer to Google Docs — a collaborative document editor embedded within the platform. You can embed board data, mention teammates, and keep project documentation alongside the work itself. It's handy for briefs, meeting notes, and SOPs, though it's not a replacement for Google Docs or Notion for heavy document users.

## Pricing: What It Actually Costs UK Businesses

This is where many small business owners hit their first surprise.

Monday.com's pricing is structured around tiers and seat counts, with a minimum of three seats on all paid plans. Pricing is listed in US dollars but billed to UK customers in GBP (check your invoice for the converted rate, which fluctuates with exchange rates). VAT is added on top for UK businesses.

At the time of writing, approximate pricing tiers are:

| Plan | Approx. price per seat/month (billed annually) | Notable inclusions |
|---|---|---|
| **Free** | £0 | Up to 2 seats only, 3 boards, limited features |
| **Basic** | ~£9/seat/month | Unlimited boards, 5GB storage, no automations |
| **Standard** | ~£12/seat/month | Timeline, Gantt, calendar views, 250 automations/month |
| **Pro** | ~£19/seat/month | Time tracking, 25,000 automations/month, private boards |
| **Enterprise** | Custom | Advanced security, compliance, dedicated support |

*Prices are approximate GBP equivalents, billed annually. Monthly billing is available at a higher rate. Always verify current pricing before purchasing.*

For a team of three on the Standard plan (the tier most small businesses actually need for automations and timeline views), you're looking at roughly £36/month billed annually — or closer to £43/month if paying monthly. That's not unreasonable for what you get, but it's also not the budget tool the ads might imply.

The free plan is effectively a trial rather than a working solution — two seats and three boards won't cover a functioning small business.

**One important note for UK buyers:** Monday.com's prices are shown excluding VAT. Add 20% to whatever you see quoted, unless you're VAT-registered and can reclaim it.

## What Monday.com Does Well

- **Visual clarity.** Boards are genuinely easy to scan. Status columns, colour coding, and multiple views make it fast to understand where things stand without digging.
- **Flexibility.** You can build almost any kind of tracking system on it. It adapts to your workflow rather than forcing you into someone else's methodology.
- **Onboarding.** The templates library is extensive and genuinely useful for getting started quickly. There's a template for client projects, content calendars, recruitment pipelines, product roadmaps, and much more.
- **Scalability.** If your team grows, Monday.com grows with you. You won't outgrow it quickly, and you won't need to migrate to a different tool as you add headcount.
- **Mobile app.** The iOS and Android apps are among the better mobile experiences in this category — not an afterthought.

---

## What Monday.com Does Less Well

- **Pricing for very small teams.** The three-seat minimum means solo operators or two-person businesses are paying for a seat they don't use. It makes the entry cost higher than competitors like ClickUp or Notion for micro-businesses.
- **Feature overload.** The breadth of the platform can be overwhelming for someone who just wants a simple to-do list or client tracker. It takes time to set up properly, and if you don't invest that time, you end up with a messy collection of half-built boards.
- **Automations cap on lower plans.** 250 automations per month on the Standard plan sounds like a lot until you have several boards each running a handful of rules. Heavier users will find themselves on the Pro plan sooner than expected.
- **No built-in time tracking on lower plans.** Time tracking is a Pro feature. For freelancers who bill by the hour, this means either upgrading or integrating a separate time-tracking tool.
- **Customer support.** Support is primarily ticket-based. Response times are generally reasonable, but there's no phone support, and live chat availability depends on your plan tier.
- **Dollar pricing and exchange rate exposure.** Costs fluctuate slightly with the GBP/USD exchange rate, which makes budgeting mildly unpredictable. Not a dealbreaker, but worth noting.

## How It Compares to Key Alternatives

**vs. Asana:** Asana has a more generous free plan (up to 15 users) and a cleaner interface for task-focused work, but fewer views and less flexibility for non-project use cases. Monday.com wins on customisation; Asana wins on simplicity and free-tier value.

**vs. ClickUp:** ClickUp packs in more features at lower price points and has a genuinely useful free tier. It's more complex to set up, and the interface can feel cluttered, but for a cost-conscious small business it's a strong competitor.

**vs. Notion:** Notion is more of a knowledge management and documentation tool that also does project tracking. It's cheaper and more flexible for document-heavy workflows, but the project management features aren't as polished as Monday.com's.

**vs. Trello:** Trello is simpler and cheaper (with a decent free tier), but it's primarily kanban-only and lacks the views, automations, and reporting depth of Monday.com. Fine for very simple task tracking; limiting for anything more complex.

## Final Recommendation

Monday.com is a well-built, genuinely useful platform for small businesses that have outgrown spreadsheets and need a proper system for managing projects, clients, and team work. The visual interface is one of the best in the category, the flexibility is real, and it scales without friction as your business grows.

But it's not the right tool for everyone. If you're a freelancer or a very small team, the pricing model works against you, and the feature set may be more than you need. In that case, ClickUp's free tier or Asana's free plan for small teams are worth looking at first.

If you do have a team of three or more and you're serious about getting organised, Monday.com is worth the investment — provided you commit to setting it up properly. Like any powerful tool, you'll get out of it what you put in. A half-built Monday.com instance is worse than a well-maintained spreadsheet.

Start with the 14-day free trial on the Pro plan (Monday.com offers this), build out one or two real boards with your actual work, and see whether it clicks for your team before committing.

## FAQ

**1. Is there a free version of Monday.com for small businesses?**
Yes, but it's very limited — two seats maximum and only three active boards. It's more useful as an extended trial than a working solution for a real business. Most small businesses will need at least the Standard plan to get meaningful value, which requires a paid subscription with a minimum of three seats.

**2. Does Monday.com work well for freelancers managing client projects?**
It can, but the three-seat minimum pricing makes it expensive for a solo operator. If you're a freelancer working alone, tools like ClickUp (with a more generous free tier) or Notion may give better value. If you collaborate regularly with contractors or subcontractors, Monday.com becomes more justifiable.

**3. Is Monday.com GDPR-compliant for UK businesses?**
Monday.com is GDPR-compliant and offers a Data Processing Agreement (DPA) for customers, which you'll want to have in place if you're storing client or employee data on the platform. Data is stored on servers in the US and/or EU depending on your settings — check your account region if data residency matters for your business.

**4. Can I use Monday.com as a CRM for my small business?**
Yes — monday CRM is a dedicated product within the platform that handles contact management, lead tracking, deal pipelines, and basic email integration. It's not as deep as HubSpot or Salesforce, but for a small business that wants CRM and project management in one place without two separate subscriptions, it's a practical option.

**5. What happens to my data if I cancel my Monday.com subscription?**
Monday.com allows you to export your board data before cancelling. Exported data comes as an Excel file. If you cancel, your account moves to read-only for a period before data is eventually deleted — the exact timeframe is outlined in their terms of service. It's worth exporting everything before you cancel rather than after.
